INTRODUCTION Soil mechanics is the science that explores how soil behaves under various conditions, helping engineers ensure the stability and safety of structures, foundations, and infrastructure projects. The realm of civil engineering leans significantly on the foundation of soil mechanics, ensuring the safety and enduring stability of a wide array of construction projects. Within this conference project, I will be referencing prominent case studies, such as the Teton Dam, the Venice Flooding, and the Transcona Elevator. These exemplify the pivotal role that soil mechanics plays in shaping the landscape of engineering.

ABSTRACT Soil mechanics is a crucial discipline for engineers, as it is central to ensuring the safety, stability, and sustainability of civil infrastructure. This abstract summarizes the key reasons why soil mechanics is pivotal for engineers.Soil mechanics guides engineers in comprehending soil deformation, bearing capacity, and settlement behaviour, critical for designing foundations, retaining walls, tunnels, and geotechnical structures. In summary, soil mechanics is pivotal in engineering, forming the foundation for safe, durable, and environmentally responsible structures. Without it, engineering projects are at risk of failure, with potentially catastrophic consequences, as we have seen in the case studies covered.
